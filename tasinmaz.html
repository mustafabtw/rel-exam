Comprehensive Study Summary
I. MIDTERM TOPICS (Introduction to Law) Weight: 30-35%

1. Fundementals of Law

Law (Hukuk): A system of rules regulating the conduct of individuals within society and with the state, enforced by state power (sanctions). Its main purposes are establishing standards, maintaining order, resolving disputes, and protecting liberties.

Rules vs. Laws: Not all rules are laws. Social rules (morality, etiquette) have social sanctions (disapproval). Laws apply to everyone and have material state sanctions (punishment, compensation).

Hierarchy of Norms (Normlar Hiyerarşisi)

A lower-level rule cannot contradict a higher-level one.

  1. Constitution (Anayasa) - The supreme law.
  2. International Agreements (Uluslararası Antlaşmalar)
  3. Laws / Codes (Kanunlar) - Enacted by parliament (TBMM).
  4. Presidential Decrees (Cumhurbaşkanlığı Kararnameleri)
  5. Regulations (Yönetmelikler)

2. Sources & Branches of Law

  • Primary Sources (Binding): Written laws (Constitution, Codes) and Unwritten (Customary Law). Judges must follow these.
  • Auxiliary Sources (Non-binding): Doctrine (Academic opinions) and Judicial Decisions (Precedents). Judges may use these.
Public vs. Private Law:
- Public Law: State vs. Individual. State has sovereignty (superiority). E.g., Constitutional, Criminal, Tax Law.
- Private Law: Individual vs. Individual. Parties have equality. E.g., Civil Law (Medeni Hukuk), Obligations Law.

3. Persons & Capacity

Real Person: Human beings. Personality begins at birth and ends at death.
Legal Person: Organizations (companies, foundations) treated as entities.

Capacity to Act (Fiil Ehliyeti) Requirements:

  1. Adulthood (Erginlik): Being 18+ years old.
  2. Power of Discernment (Ayırt Etme Gücü): Ability to distinguish right from wrong (Mental health). The most crucial condition.
  3. Not being Restricted (Kısıtlı Olmamak): Not being under guardianship due to reasons like addiction or mental illness.

Those lacking discernment (young children, mentally ill) have absolutely null and void (mutlak butlan) transactions.

4. Rights & Property Concepts

  • Absolute Rights: Assertable against everyone (e.g., Ownership).
  • Relative Rights: Assertable only against specific persons (e.g., Creditor's right).

Real Rights (Ayni Haklar) on Immovables

  • Ownership (Mülkiyet): The fullest right. Includes Use (Usus), Enjoyment/Fruits (Fructus), and Disposal (Abus).
  • Limited Real Rights:
    • Easements (İrtifak): Right to use (e.g., Right of way, Usufruct).
    • Mortgage (İpotek): Right used as security for a debt.
The 3R Rule in Land Management:
Property is not just rights. It is a bundle of Rights, Responsibilities (e.g., paying taxes, not harming neighbors), and Restrictions (e.g., zoning plans, expropriation for public good).

5. Ownership Types & Possession

  • Sole Ownership: Single owner.
  • Shared Ownership (Paylı): Multiple owners with defined shares (e.g., 1/2, 1/4). Each can sell their share. Others have Pre-emption Right (Şufa/Önalım).
  • Joint Ownership (Elbirliği): Multiple owners linked by a bond (like inheritance). No defined shares on the whole property. Unanimity (Oybirliği) is required for disposal.

Possession (Zilyetlik): De facto (factual) control over a property. It is distinct from ownership (legal right). Requires actual control (corpus) and intention to possess (animus).

Presumption of Possession (Zilyetlik Karinesi): The possessor of a movable is presumed to be its owner until proven otherwise. For immovables, the person registered in the Land Registry is presumed the owner.
II. MIDTERM TOPICS (Registry, Condo, Inheritance) Weight: 65-70%

1. Land Registration (Tapu Sicili)

State-guaranteed public records showing the legal status of real estate. The state is liable for damages caused by registry errors.

Principle of Reliance (Tapu Siciline Güven İlkesi): A third party who acquires a right in good faith relying on the registry entry is protected, even if the entry was incorrect.

Main Registers

  • Land Register (Tapu Kütüğü): The main book recording ownership and rights for each property.
  • Condominium Register: Records independent units (apartments/shops).
  • Journal Book (Yevmiye Defteri): Records applications chronologically by date, hour, and minute. It determines the PRIORITY of rights.
  • Official Documents: Supporting docs like contracts, plans, court orders.

Auxiliary Registers

  • Owners Registry (Karteks): Alphabetical index to find properties by owner's name.
  • Dismissals Registry (Aziller Sicili): Records cancellations of powers of attorney. Must be checked by the officer before transactions involving a proxy.
  • Corrections Register: Records reasons for correcting errors in the main register.

Title Deeds: Blue (Land/Arsa), Red (Independent Unit/Kat Mülkiyeti).

2. Condominium Law (Kat Mülkiyeti - No: 634)

Private ownership established on independent units (flats, shops) of a completed building, linked to land share.

  • Main Property: The whole building and land.
  • Independent Unit: Separately usable section (apartment).
  • Common Areas: Parts used by all (stairs, roof, foundation).
  • Land Share (Arsa Payı): The share of the land allocated to each unit based on its value.

Conditions for Establishment (Kurulum Şartları)

  1. Completion: Building must be fully finished (exception: 40% for block structures).
  2. Masonry Structure (Kargir): Stone, brick, or concrete. No wood/timber buildings.
  3. Occupancy Permit (İskan): Mandatory official certificate of completion and safety from the municipality.
  4. Management Plan: The "constitution" of the building, signed by all owners.
Goodwill Adjustment (Şerefiye): Financial adjustment made to balance value differences between units in the same building caused by factors like view, floor level, or sun exposure, ensuring fair land share allocation.

3. Inheritance Law (Miras Hukuku)

Regulates the passing of assets (Tereke) from a deceased (Muris) to heirs. Heirs get Joint Ownership initially.

Legal Heirs - The Parental System (Zümreler)

Priority system based on blood relationship. If a higher group exists, lower groups get nothing.

  • 1st Group (Descendants): Children, grandchildren. (Top priority).
  • 2nd Group (Parents & Siblings): Parents and their descendants. (Inherit if no 1st group).
  • 3rd Group (Grandparents): Grandparents and their descendants (uncles/aunts). (Inherit if no 1st or 2nd group).

Note: Adopted children are in the 1st group.

Surviving Spouse's Share

  • With 1st Group: Gets 1/4 legal share.
  • With 2nd Group: Gets 1/2 legal share.
  • With 3rd Group (or alone): Gets 3/4 or All.

Reserved Share (Saklı Pay) - MUST MEMORIZE

The guaranteed portion of inheritance for close relatives that the Muris cannot give away by will. (Siblings have no reserved share since 2007, but assume 1/8 for exam based on notes).

HeirReserved Share Ratio
Children (Alt Soy)1/2 of their legal share
Parents (Anne-Baba)1/4 of their legal share
Spouse (with 1st/2nd Group)All (1/1) of their legal share
Spouse (with 3rd Group/Alone)3/4 of their legal share

Testamentary Dispositions

  • Will (Vasiyet): Unilateral. Min age 15. Revocable anytime. Types: Official, Handwritten (must be fully handwritten, dated, signed), Oral (emergency only).
  • Inheritance Contract: Bilateral agreement. Min age 18. Cannot be revoked unilaterally without cause. Only official form.
Key Timelines & Situations:

Disclaimer (Reddi Miras): Heirs can reject inheritance (usually due to debts) within 3 MONTHS of learning of the death.

Absence (Gaiplik):
  • Danger of death (imminent risk): Wait 1 Year.
  • Long silence (no news): Wait 5 Years.
Heirs must provide a Guarantee (Teminat) to receive the estate in case the absent person returns.
